ABOUT THE EVENT

Be Part of the First MQ Summit!

Message queues are the backbone of modern distributed systems, ensuring seamless communication, scalability, and resilience.

Building on the success of RabbitMQ Summit, MQ Summit now brings together professionals working with a wide range of messaging systems, including RabbitMQ, Kafka, NATS, Apache Pulsar, Azure Messaging Brokers, Amazon SQS, IBM MQ, and more. Whether you're developing event-driven architectures, optimizing system performance, or making strategic technology decisions, this conference offers valuable insights from industry experts.

Why Attend?
A. Deep Dive into Messaging – Learn about RabbitMQ, Kafka, Pulsar, Azure, NATS, Amazon SQS, Google Pub/Sub, and more.
B. Technical & Strategic Insights – Whether you build with MQs or decide which to adopt, gain knowledge from experts.
C. Network with Industry Leaders – Connect with peers, technology creators, and business decision-makers.
D. Real-World Case Studies – See how messaging solutions power mission-critical applications.

CALL FOR TALKS

CFT is closed for 2025!

MQ Summit is where messaging experts, developers, and decision-makers come together to explore the future of message queues and event-driven architectures. We’re looking for insightful talks that dive into real-world use cases, best practices, and the latest advancements in messaging technologies. CFT closes: 6 July (extended) 2025

What We’re Looking For:

  • Deep dives into messaging systems like RabbitMQ, Kafka, Pulsar, NATS, Amazon SQS, Google Cloud Pub/Sub, and more
  • Case studies of large-scale implementations, performance tuning, and troubleshooting
  • Innovations in messaging, event-driven architectures, and hybrid cloud strategies
  • Security, observability, and best practices for running reliable messaging infrastructure
  • Tooling, interoperability, and evolving standards in the MQ ecosystem
  • Trends in how messaging tech is enabling:
    • Cloud-to-edge architectures
    • AI-powered systems
    • Smart manufacturing
    • Connected vehicles
  • Exploring messaging patterns in a point-to-point centric world:
    • Pub/Sub, M:N, push vs. pull
    • Messaging core vs. HTTP shell
    • Art of the possible with modern message exchange approaches
